Types of Driving Licenses (Alberta)

Class 7 — Learner’s License

For people learning to drive. Must drive with an experienced driver. Rules: no driving late, no alcohol, limited passengers.

Class 5 — Full Driver’s License

After Class 7 you can take a road test to get Class 5-GDL. After 2 clean years you get a full Class 5.

Class 6 & Commercial

Class 6 — motorcycles. Commercial: Class 1,2,3,4 (large vehicles).

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting Your License

Step 1 — Class 7 Knowledge Test

  • Minimum age: 14 (Alberta)
  • Test includes road rules & signs

Step 2 — Practice Driving

  • Drive with a fully licensed adult

Step 3 — Take Class 5-GDL Test

  • After 1 year with Class 7
  • Drive alone but follow GDL rules

Step 4 — Full Class 5 License

  • After 2 clean years with GDL

Moving to a new country comes with many challenges, especially when it comes to understanding a different driving system. Many newcomers from India, Pakistan, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Middle East, and Africa find Canadian driving rules very different from what they are used to. This website has been created to give you clear, step-by-step, newcomer-friendly driving guidance,ETC
Roundabouts and four-way stops are another challenge. New drivers get confused about who goes first. This site provides diagrams and simple rules: first car goes first, the right-hand rule, yielding in roundabouts, and proper signaling. By learning these tips, newcomers become confident and safe drivers who protect themselves, their families, and others on the road.
